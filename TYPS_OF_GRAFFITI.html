Symbolic, cosmetic, graphic: tattoos (a depiction of a specific person or item). Tattoos are for identification, beauty, and medicine.

T A T T O O H I S T O R Y

Tattoo History

In early Greek and Roman times (eighth to sixth century BCE), tattooing was associated with barbarians. The Greeks learned tattooing from the Persians, and used it to mark slaves and criminals so they could be identified if they tried to escape. The Romans in turn adopted this practise from the Greeks.

W H Y T A T T O O ?

Why Tattoo

People get tattoos for many reasons: attention, self-expression, artistic freedom, rebellion, a visual display of a personal narrative, reminders of spiritual or cultural traditions, sexual motivation, addiction, identification with a group, or even drunken impulsiveness.

MS13: World's most Dangerous Gang

The MS13 gang, aka Mara Salvatrucha 13, is one of the most violently dangerous gangs in the United States—and one of the most organized.
The MS13 gang has cliques, or factions, located throughout the United States and is unique in that it retains ties to its El Salvador counterparts.

Does Tattoo Related to Graffiti?

Graffiti, considered an expressive curse of the lower class, gravitates naturally to the application of tattoos. Graffiti and tattoos are both considered forms of drawings or inscriptions outside of an archaeological context—graffiti falling somewhat into a social nature, with tattoos being more personal.
