Graffiti

the artist Cornbread was the first person to do graffiti. Most people think that Darryl McCray, who is better known as "Cornbread," was the first graffiti artist. In 1965, Cornbread was a 12-year-old boy in Philadelphia who liked to write his name on every surface he could find.

Cornbread started a trend that spread quickly and became very popular. Cornbread is best known for the elephant he tagged at the Philadelphia Zoo, which caused a lot of trouble. Cornbread wrote "Cornbread Lives" on both sides of an elephant that was still alive because he was wrongly said to be dead. Graffiti artists today, like Alec Monopoly, take their inspiration from Cornbread and tag a wider range of things. (Graffiti Art: What Is Graffiti? Is Graffiti Art? - Eden Gallery, 2021)

G R A F F I T I I S V A N D A L I S M ?

Graffiti is writing or drawings on a wall or other surface, usually as a form of an artistic expression, without permission and within public view. Furthermore, where is the line between art and vandalism? Ironically, if you look up vandalism, the first picture to pop up is a graffiti painting.

When people in our society see any form of graffiti, they shut it down. We have told ourselves that spray painted art is vandalism. In reality, a spraypaint can is just a tool that artists use to create an art piece. Like painting tools, graffiti is a tool for the mass communications of the world. It can get a person’s opinions and voices heard in an anonymous way, and get people talking.
